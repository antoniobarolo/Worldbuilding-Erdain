The Planet

Erdain is a rocky planet with similar habitable conditions to Earth, though with a much more intense climate. The main differences are that in finds itself in a Type-S Binary Star System and that it has an axial tilt of 58 degrees. Also, the length of a day and an year are shorter than Earth's, its atmosphere is thicke, having especially a higher proportion of water, oxygen, and CO2 than Earth's. Erdain is also smaller, with a radius similar to Venus, and the gravity is weaker there.

Due to its high axial tilt, the planet suffers with several days of continuous sunlight or night-time even at low latitudes, making the warmer portions of Erdain's surface be at the poles, while the equator is the coldest. This causes the polar and tropical climates to switch positions in comparison to Earth, although these climates and their respective biomes function very differently in Erdain.

By result of these factors Erdain also has a warmer overall temperature than current day Earth, and is distinct for its way stronger air and ocean currents. Due to this, the planet is also known as the Stormy Sphere.

Formation and Composition

Erdain formed in an equivalent fashion to Earth, from the collision of planetoids around Narháera. A little later the Stormy Sphere also suffered larger collisions that formed its Moons.

Being considerably younger than Earth, Erdain has a higher inner temperature, though its smaller size atenuates this difference a bit. And by a small margin, Erdain has proportinally more iron than Earth, and a thicker inner core. These factors provide a stronger magnetic field that protects it from the close rays of Narháera and the highly radioactive rays of Ghaláera.

Other factors that contributes to this magnetic field are its lower gravity and size, making Erdain's crust thicker and its tectonic activity stronger. On that note, the planet also has greater amplitude of relief.
